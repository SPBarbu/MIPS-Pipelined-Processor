The mips green sheet states the structure of an instruction's bits and gives a listing of the instruction's
opcodes themselves. This document tries to summarize and simplify it.

All opcodes are placed from bits 31 downto 26 (6 most signicant bits) in the 32bit instruction word for all instructions. 
Other parts of the instruction vary depending on if it reads from register (R) or immediate (I) values.

For register instructions, the opcode is not always enough and you also need to refer to the "funct" field which is bits
5 downto 0 (6 least significant bits of the instruction) in order to decode.

Some jumps have their own address format but the only thing that is imporatant is the opcode.

For an illustration of how each instruction is formatted in an assembly program see opcode.asm.

Instruction structure:
Register:
|--opcode--|---rs---|---rt---|---rd---|--shamt--|--funct--|
31       26 25    21 20    16 15    11 10      6 5        0

Immediate:
|--opcode--|---rs---|---rt---|----------immediate---------|
31       26 25    21 20    16 15                          0

Jump:
|--opcode--|--------------------address-------------------|
31       26 25                                            0
